L-sit → press to handstand
The goal. Phase 4+. Year 2–4.
Goal
Requires all six prerequisites simultaneously — strength, balance, flexibility, compression
The L-sit provides compression to press legs overhead. The handstand provides the destination.
The straddle press (legs apart) is slightly easier than pike press (legs together) — train that first
On parallettes: slightly easier than floor due to wrist angle and clearance

Freestanding handstand
10+ sec controlled hold
Phase 4 · Month 18–30
The hardest prerequisite. Requires daily 10–15 min wall + freestanding practice — separate from strength training
Straight body, ears between arms, eyes forward, fingertip pressure for balance
Wall handstand → chest-to-wall HS → kick up to freestanding → hold progression
Most people need 12–24 months of consistent daily practice

Full L-sit 10+ sec
Legs parallel to floor
Phase 3–4 · Month 9–18
Arms locked straight, legs fully extended horizontal, body in compression
Requires both hip flexor strength AND hamstring flexibility — both must be present
If hamstrings are tight, you can have the hip flexor strength and still not hold it
Build from tuck → single-leg extend → full. See L-sit progression card.

Wall handstand 30+ sec
Chest-to-wall, straight body
Phase 2–3 · Month 6–12
Face the wall, walk hands in close, body straight (not banana)
Chest-to-wall forces proper body line — not the arched "banana" handstand
Hold 30+ sec with straight body before progressing to kicking up
Practice: kick up, hold, controlled descent. 5–10 attempts per day session.

Tuck press to HS (floor)
First pressing movement
Phase 3 · Month 12–20
From tuck L-sit: shift weight forward over hands and press legs overhead
This is the pressing pattern — the same movement as the full skill but with tucked legs
Requires: solid tuck L-sit + solid shoulder strength from the main program
Parallettes make this significantly easier than floor (more clearance)

Single-leg L-sit
One leg extended, one tucked
Phase 2–3 · Month 6–12
One leg fully extended horizontal. One leg tucked.
10 sec per side. Builds the compression and hip flexor strength asymmetrically
Direct bridge to the full L-sit — easier than both legs extended
If you can hold this for 15 sec each side, you're close to the full L-sit
