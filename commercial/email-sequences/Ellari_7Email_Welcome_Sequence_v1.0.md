---

The thing about drift is that it's invisible until it's catastrophic.

You don't notice the first five names that don't quite fit the aesthetic. You don't notice when the AI generates a character detail that contradicts the one you wrote six months ago. You don't notice when your visual system starts pulling in three different directions.

Then one day you look at the whole thing and something feels wrong. You can't say exactly what. It just doesn't hold together the way it used to.

That's drift.

There are five types:

**Name drift** — Your names don't share phonetic DNA. New names feel like they're from a different universe.

**Lore drift** — Facts change between sessions. The AI remembers rules you've changed and rules you haven't changed simultaneously.

**Tone drift** — The voice of your world is different in different documents. One section sounds formal, another sounds casual. Neither sounds like you.

**Visual drift** — Your symbols, colors, and marks are pulling in different directions. The aesthetic has no spine.

**AI prompt drift** — You haven't given your AI a canon brief, so it's filling in the gaps with pattern-matching. It's confidently wrong.

Most people have at least two of these active right now.

## EMAIL 5

### Subject [A]: Two names for the same thing. Here's why that matters.
### Subject [B]: Crown Script vs. Canon Speech: how to keep your myth from breaking your language.

---

Here is a problem I see constantly:

Someone builds a beautiful mythological name — rich, symbolic, layered — and then has to use it as a functional brand name. It looks incredible in a title. It sounds horrible in a URL. It fails trademark clearance. It's impossible to pronounce.

The fix is a distinction I call Crown Script vs. Canon Speech.

**Crown Script** is the visual, mythic, symbolic layer. It holds the beautiful names, the titles, the glyphs, the ceremonial forms. It can be complex. It can be layered. It can be difficult to pronounce. Its job is to feel powerful.

**Canon Speech** is the functional layer. It holds the names that go on products, domains, and trademarks. It's governed, precise, and pronounceable. Its job is to work.

In the Ellari system, this looks like:

- Crown Script: *Aetherion* (the mythic universe name) — beautiful, complex, not a trademark
- Canon Speech: *Eterion* (the functional language name) — governed, clear, ownable
- Brand: *Ellari* — the commercial parent, scores 95/100 on the naming matrix

Same system. Two layers. Each doing its job.

When you separate these two layers, you stop making the same impossible demand: that your mythology also be a usable business name. It doesn't have to be. You can have both.
