### AET-DEC-009
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** Formal ruling on /en/ phoneme — IPA correction
**Ruling:** The vowel e in all Aetherion words is always /e/ — pure front mid-closed vowel, as in Spanish "el." The transcription /ɛn/ appearing in the Section 10 Plain Voice example "En var-a sa sel." is a transcription error. Correct transcription: /en ˈva.ɾa sa sel/. The form /ɛn/ does not represent a valid Aetherion vowel. This ruling closes all potential arguments for vowel reduction in the word "en" based on that example.
**Affected documents:** Pronunciation Guide Section 10, Master Dictionary v0.5 (AET-CORE-001 entry), audio production scripts.
**Status:** LOCKED. No deviation permitted.

### AET-DEC-010
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** Onset cluster position specification — fr and vr
**Ruling:** The clusters fr and vr are onset clusters only. They may appear only in the onset (C)(C) position of the syllable structure (C)(C)V(C). They may not appear at cross-boundary positions within multi-syllable words where they would create a coda+onset ambiguity. In any Aetherion word, if fr or vr appears at a syllable boundary, the boundary must be drawn so that both consonants are in the onset of the following syllable, not split across coda and onset.
**Example:** e-vres /eˈvɾes/ — syllabified as e.vres, where vr is the onset of the second syllable. NOT ev.res.
**Status:** LOCKED.

### AET-DEC-011
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** y and w glide policy
**Ruling:** y /j/ and w /w/ are listed as allowed consonants in the Aetherion inventory. Zero canonical lexical instances currently exist. Any proposed word using y or w requires additional review as a pioneering form, subject to the full CCR (Canon Change Request) process. The first word using each glide establishes the canonical pattern for all subsequent words using that consonant. Until the first y-initial and w-initial words are formally approved, these consonants are classified as "ALLOWED IN PRINCIPLE — NO CANONICAL INSTANCES." They may not be used in operator lines, product names, or core vocabulary without a formal decision record.
**Status:** ACTIVE — awaiting first canonical instance proposals.

### AET-DEC-012
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** del-te syllabification formal ruling
**Ruling:** The completive form of del is syllabified as del.te — CVC.CV. The /l/ closes the first syllable; /t/ opens the second. No consonant cluster forms at the syllable boundary. del-te is phonologically valid and canonically approved. The conditional phrasing in Pronunciation Guide Section 5 ("de-lte would be invalid if it created an illegal cluster") is clarified: del-te does not create an illegal cluster under the established syllabification, and the conditional concern is resolved. del-te remains ATTESTED.
**IPA:** /ˈdel.te/
**Status:** LOCKED.

### AET-DEC-013
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** El'rasha notation correction
**Ruling:** The apostrophe in the form El'rasha Veinon Kaltra is removed from all official documents. In standard IPA, the apostrophe (') denotes ejective consonants — a sound category not present in Aetherion's phoneme inventory. Its use creates a misreading risk. The intended meaning is a short word-boundary pause (~380ms). Official IPA transcription: [el ˈɾa.ʃa ˈve.non ˈkal.tɾa] with a word boundary marker between el and rasha, not an apostrophe. In plain text, the form is written as: El rasha Veinon Kaltra (space, no apostrophe).
**Affected documents:** All pronunciation guides, audio scripts, operator documents referencing this phrase.
**Status:** LOCKED.

### AET-DEC-014
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** Neutral domain formally closed to new lexical entries
**Ruling:** The neutral domain contains only the existing canonical function words: en, ta, sa, de, vi, fa, lo, na, la, ki, kan. No new content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) may be assigned to the neutral domain. Neutral domain = particles and function words only. Any proposed word that cannot be assigned to identity, role, role-field, or triad domains may not be assigned to neutral as a default or workaround. Proposals lacking a valid domain assignment must either receive a new domain through a formal ELEOS expansion (Impact Score 9-10, requires extreme restraint) or be rejected. The neutral domain is not an overflow bucket.
**Rationale:** Without this ruling, "neutral" becomes a soft escape valve for domain-bypass requests.
**Status:** LOCKED.

### AET-DEC-015
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** High Voice trill boundary and no-exceptions rule
**Ruling:** The High Voice trill (r pronounced as /r/ rather than /ɾ/) is a delivery style variation only. It does not constitute a phonological exception, a new phoneme, or a precedent for phonological variations in any register. The single sentence "High Voice trill = style only" does not create a template for other style-based phonological additions. The rule is: register shifts (Plain Voice → High Voice) affect delivery, syntax, and pace. They do not affect the phoneme inventory. No phonological exceptions exist in any register. No new phonemes may be introduced regardless of register or delivery style.
**Rationale:** Prevents "in register X, phoneme Y is allowed as style" template exploitation.
**Status:** LOCKED.

### AET-DEC-016
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** Crown Script Rejected category established
**Ruling:** A "Crown Script Rejected" status is formally added to the Crown Script classification system. Full Crown Script status hierarchy:
- Crown Locked: Fully approved visual form
- Crown Preferred: Recommended title or seal form
- Crown Candidate: Potentially useful, not finalized
- Crown Public: External or lore-facing only
- Crown Rejected: Formally barred from Crown Script
Crown Script Rejected criteria: (1) creates visual confusion with Canon Speech forms, (2) introduces naming patterns that undermine the Canon Speech/Crown Script separation, (3) aesthetically weak in the context of the established visual system, (4) conflicts with the LL monogram doctrine or Ellari visual lockup system, (5) approved for explicit rejection by decision record.
**Current Crown Script Rejected forms:** Ellarii (double-i confusion), Elarri (doubled-r confusion), Aethari (ae+th drift risk), Ellari Queen of Aetherion (too generic fantasy).
**Status:** ACTIVE — Crown Rejected list may be extended as needed.

### AET-DEC-017
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** Held trigger conditions for Ellariem, Ellarien, Ellaries
**Ruling:** The three held forms Ellariem, Ellarien, and Ellaries (Ellari + -em, Ellari + -en, Ellari + -es) remain held in Canon Speech. They are approved as Crown Script candidates only. These forms may be reviewed for Canon Speech promotion only when ALL of the following conditions are met: (1) A formal Proper Name Affixation Policy document has been written and approved as a separate document from the current grammar rules. (2) The policy defines which proper names may receive affixes and under what semantic constraints. (3) At least three other proper-name affix examples are attested in Canon Speech, establishing a pattern. Until these conditions are met, Ellariem, Ellarien, and Ellaries are Crown Script layer forms only. They may appear in visual design, titles, and mythological text but not in Canon Speech operators, dictionary entries, or pronunciation teaching materials.
**Status:** HELD with formal trigger conditions. Not rejected. Not orphaned.

### AET-DEC-018
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Decision:** Var-a en sa rola vi sa triem repositioned as primary diagnostic operator
**Ruling:** The operator "Var-a en sa rola vi sa triem." (I see the role in the triad) is repositioned from a general diagnostic option to the PRIMARY FIRST OPERATOR in any role-exit or triad-exit sequence. Rationale: all other operators in the role and triad domains are declarative (they state what is true after analysis). Var-a is the investigative entry point — the act of seeing what is present before naming it. The operator bank documentation must reflect this repositioning: Var-a en sa rola vi sa triem. is listed first in any role or triad sequence, preceding the declarative operators. This establishes the sequence structure: Diagnostic (Var-a) → Declarative (Lo es / mel-a) → Action (del-te / dam-te).
**Sequence model:**
- Step 1 — Var-a en sa rola vi sa triem. (I see the role in the triad.)
- Step 2 — En mel-a ki sa triem es luma/dala. (I classify the valence.)
- Step 3 — Lo es rola de Ellari. (I refuse the role.)
- Step 4 — En del-te rola. Ellari. (I unbound it.)
**Status:** APPROVED. Affects operator bank, Sound Key bridge, and competition day protocol.
